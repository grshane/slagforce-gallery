Original Design — 18° Lander

The baseline design with an 18° landing slope. This was the initial spec — flat and forgiving but with a harsh 51° impact angle relative to the slope.

Landing angle: 18° Impact relative to slope: ~51° Required speed: ~7.1 mph Sweet spot: 14' out

25° Lander

First steeper option. Moderately reduces the impact angle — a step in the right direction but still quite firm on landing.

Landing angle: 25° Impact relative to slope: ~45° Required speed: ~7.0 mph Sweet spot: 9' out

30° Lander

Good middle ground. Noticeably softer landing than 25°, with a manageable slope for the build.

Landing angle: 30° Impact relative to slope: ~40° Required speed: ~6.9 mph Sweet spot: 9' out

35° Lander

Aggressive but effective. Significant improvement in landing quality — the slope absorbs much more of the rider's velocity.

Landing angle: 35° Impact relative to slope: ~36° Required speed: ~6.8 mph Sweet spot: 9' out

Option E — 40° Lander, 4' Case Pad, 12' Sweet Spot

The recommended optimized design. 40° lander with the sweet spot pushed to 12' and an extended 4' case pad for safety margin. Best landing quality of the non-roll-down options.

Landing angle: 40° Impact relative to slope: ~29° Required speed: ~8.5 mph Sweet spot: 12' out Case pad: 4' Total impact speed: ~22.5 mph Perpendicular impact: ~10.3 mph (like a 3.6' flat drop)

Roll-Down v1 — Exits Bridge End

First iteration of the side roll-down concept. The ramp exits from the bridge end and curves back to the lander. Superseded by the trail-fork version.

Landing angle: 40° Roll-down: 38° slope, 14' run Roll-down exits: bridge end

Trail Fork Roll-Down — Final Design

The roll-down forks off the main trail before the bridge (40° between trail and bridge). Riders choose their line at the fork: bridge for the drop, or main trail for the rollable ramp that curves down and merges into the lander. Both lines share the same lander and runout.

Landing angle: 40° Roll-down: 38° slope, 14' run, 3' wide Trail/bridge angle: 40° Roll-down exits: trail fork (before bridge) Drop speed: ~8.5 mph takeoff, ~22.5 mph at landing Roll-down no-brakes speed: ~18 mph

180° Berm — Option A (Relaxed)

Placed 19 feet from the lander transition. Gives riders a comfortable braking zone to scrub from ~17 mph down to ~12 mph before entering. The 44° bank angle is steep but very rideable — a natural match for the skill level of anyone hitting this drop.

Radius: 10' Turn arc: 180° Bank angle: 44° Lip height: ~4' 2" Riding surface: 6' wide Entry speed: ~12 mph Gap from lander: 19' Berm center: 29' from lander

180° Berm — Option B (Punchy)

Pulled in to 15 feet from the lander. Riders enter hotter at ~14 mph, requiring a steeper 53° bank. Less braking time, more G-force in the turn — a more aggressive, punchier ride that keeps the energy flowing.

Radius: 10' Turn arc: 180° Bank angle: 53° Lip height: ~4' 10" Riding surface: 6' wide Entry speed: ~14 mph Gap from lander: 15' Berm center: 25' from lander
